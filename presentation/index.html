The Problem

Real patient data
can't be shared.

HIPAA regulations, IRB restrictions, and hospital data sharing agreements lock away the most valuable medical datasets in the world — making it nearly impossible to train ML models on real EHR data outside of a single institution.

Synthetic data generation offers a way out. But only if the synthetic records are actually statistically faithful and useful.

The Approach

Four generators. One benchmark.

Gaussian Copula
Statistical baseline
Fits marginal distributions per column, then models correlations via multivariate Gaussian. Closed-form, interpretable, trains in seconds.
CTGAN
Conditional GAN
Generator battles discriminator to produce realistic rows. Mode-specific normalization handles multi-modal distributions. 300 epochs.
TVAE
Variational autoencoder
Encodes rows into a latent space, decodes to produce synthetic samples. Good at capturing overall structure. 300 epochs.
Tabular Diffusion
Custom DDPM — built from scratch
Learns to reverse a 200-step Gaussian noising process. 3.34M param MLP denoiser with sinusoidal timestep embeddings and residual blocks.

All four trained in parallel on AWS SageMaker · ml.m5.4xlarge · outputs to S3

The Journey

Three rounds of
training to get it right.

Run 1 — Failed
Discriminator AUROC = 1.0 on all models — all data easily spotted as fake
Found: str.contains(regex=True) silently zeroed out 7 condition columns. "Body mass index 30+" treated as a regex pattern.
Found: TVAE collapsed race/ethnicity columns to all-zero due to binary features treated as numerical.
Run 2 — Closer
Discriminator still 1.0 — but metrics improved after regex fix
Found: encounter_inpatient KS = 0.677 — 90%+ of patients have zero inpatient visits. Zero-inflated distributions are a hard ceiling for all generators.
Found: Synthetic DECEASED rate 14–16% vs real 19.5% — generators under-sample minority class.
Run 3 — Best results
Applied all fixes + added Tabular Diffusion
Applied: log1p transform on 6 zero-inflated columns. Post-hoc class rebalancing to match real positive rate.
Result: Diffusion KS 0.050, Wasserstein 0.034, utility AUROC 0.9906 vs real 0.9926.

Takeaways

What we learned.

01
Diffusion models are in a different class. The correlation structure distance of 0.503 vs 1.65–3.25 for SDV models isn't a marginal win — it's a fundamental architectural difference in how well the model captures inter-feature relationships.
02
Data quality dominated the first two runs. A single wrong default (regex=True) zeroed out 7 condition columns and invalidated everything. Clean data engineering mattered more than model choice until the third run.
03
Synthetic-only training reaches 99.8% of real-data performance. Tabular Diffusion synthetic records are good enough to replace real EHR data for downstream ML — and can be shared freely with zero HIPAA risk.
