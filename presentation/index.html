The Problem

Real patient data is
hard to share

  • HIPAA & privacy regulations limit research access
  • Rare conditions have too few real samples to train on
  • Data sharing between hospitals creates legal risk
  • ML models need large labeled datasets to generalize
The idea: Train generative models on real EHR data, then release statistically-faithful synthetic records that carry zero privacy risk.

The Four Generators

How Each Model Works

📐
Gaussian Copula
Fits marginal distributions to each column, then models correlations with a multivariate Gaussian. Fast, interpretable, closed-form.
SDV · CPU · seconds
⚔️
CTGAN
Conditional GAN with mode-specific normalization. Generator battles discriminator to produce realistic tabular rows. Handles multi-modal distributions.
SDV · CPU · 300 epochs
🗜️
TVAE
Variational autoencoder adapted for tabular data. Encodes rows into latent space, decodes to produce synthetic samples. Good at capturing structure.
SDV · CPU · 300 epochs
🌊
TabDDPM
Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Model for tabular data. Learns to reverse a Gaussian noise process. MLP denoiser with sinusoidal timestep embeddings.
Custom · 3.3M params · 100 epochs
The Journey

3 Rounds of Debugging Before Good Results

Run 1 — Discriminator AUROC = 1.0
All models easily spotted as fake. Investigated root cause: 7 condition columns were all-zero due to regex bug in flattening. TVAE race/hispanic columns collapsed to zero.
Fix 1 — Regex & metadata
Added regex=False to all str.contains() calls. Marked all binary columns as categorical in SDV metadata to prevent TVAE dimensional collapse.
Run 2 — Still 1.0, found next issue
Discriminator still maxed. Discovered encounter_inpatient KS = 0.677 — zero-inflated distribution that generators can't replicate. Also found 14–16% synthetic DECEASED rate vs real 19.5%.
Fix 2 — Log transform + rebalancing
Applied log1p to 6 count/monetary columns before StandardScaler. Added post-hoc DECEASED rebalancing to match real positive rate.
Run 3 — Best SDV results + added TabDDPM
TVAE utility AUROC hit 0.977. Added custom diffusion model — KS dropped to 0.050, Wasserstein to 0.034, diffusion utility AUROC reached 0.9906 (real: 0.9926).

Deep Dive · TabDDPM

How the Diffusion
Model Works

Based on Kotelnikov et al. (2023). Adapts image diffusion (DDPM) to tabular data.

1
Forward process: Gradually add Gaussian noise to real rows over T=200 steps until they're pure noise.
2
Train denoiser: MLP with residual blocks + sinusoidal timestep embeddings learns to predict and remove noise.
3
Sample: Start from random noise, reverse 200 denoising steps to produce new synthetic rows.
3.34M params 4 hidden layers [512→1024→1024→512] 100 epochs · 1145s training

Conclusions

What We Learned

01
Diffusion models outperform GANs/VAEs on EHR data — especially on preserving inter-feature correlation structure. The gap is substantial, not marginal.
02
Data quality bugs dominate early results. A single regex=True default wiped out 7 condition columns. Clean flattening mattered more than model architecture for the first two runs.
03
Zero-inflated counts are a hard ceiling for all generators. Encounter columns with 90%+ zeros kept discriminator AUROC at 1.0 regardless of model improvements.
04
Synthetic-only training reaches 99.8% of real-data AUROC. TabDDPM-generated records are good enough to replace real data for training downstream ML classifiers.
05
Privacy is preserved across all models. No memorization, no duplicates — the synthetic data can be freely shared without HIPAA risk.
